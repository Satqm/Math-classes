Why Q, K, V?

Q — Query
"What am I looking for?"
K — Key
"What do I have to offer?"
V — Value
"Here's my actual info."

Q,K,V Vectors

5

Attention Scores — Q·K/√d

Higher score = stronger relevance. We divide by √d (√4=2) to keep numbers stable.

Attention Probabilities (Softmax)

Softmax turns scores into percentages that sum to 100% — "how much to focus on each word".

How Q&A and Reasoning Work

LLMs don't "understand" — they predict the next token based on learned patterns.

Direct Answering
Question → Answer

Hard jump. Often fails on complex tasks.
Chain-of-Thought
Question → Step 1 → Step 2 → Answer

Each step becomes context for the next prediction. Much stronger.
This is why prompting matters. The better you structure your prompt, the better the model can pattern-match to high-quality training sequences.
